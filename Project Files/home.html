Blood Cell Classification

What is Blood Cell Classification?

Blood cell classification is used in medical diagnostics to identify and categorize white blood cells (WBCs) from microscopic images. Accurate classification helps detect infections, immune disorders, and blood cancers like leukemia.

Types of White Blood Cells:

  • Neutrophils: First responders to bacterial infections. Multi-lobed nucleus with light granules.
  • Lymphocytes: Regulate immune response. Large round nucleus with thin cytoplasm.
  • Monocytes: Become macrophages to clean debris. Kidney-shaped nucleus with blue-gray cytoplasm.
  • Eosinophils: Fight parasites and involved in allergies. Bi-lobed nucleus with red-orange granules.
  • Basophils: Release histamine during allergic reactions. Dark purple granules often obscure the nucleus.

This web tool uses deep learning to classify these five types of WBCs from uploaded images, assisting in faster and more reliable medical analysis.
